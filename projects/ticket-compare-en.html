Side Project · CASE STUDY

Ticket & Pass Comparison

An AI-powered travel-ticket comparison tool — plan your itinerary by drag-and-drop and the system computes the most cost-effective ticket combination in near real time, cutting the choice paralysis and budget anxiety of independent travellers.

In development · MVP Solo PM · AI-assisted build Google Gemini API Search Grounding i18n 中/EN/日

ProblemThe problem

When planning a trip, independent travellers face a bewildering mix of attraction tickets, city passes, and transit cards. They spend hours comparing options yet still aren't sure they've made the most economical choice — and that uncertainty creates real budget anxiety that drags down the whole experience.

Core insight
General-purpose AI (ChatGPT / Gemini) can chat about itineraries, but lacks three things: real-time price data, a visual planning interface, and a purpose-built comparison engine. That gap is the opportunity.

The DecisionWhy "Ticket Comparison"

This is one focused slice of a broader vision ("Universal Travel Helper"). I used a CRISPE evaluation to weigh three options on "user value × technical feasibility × cost" — and, as a solo PM with limited resources, chose the MVP that solves one high-value pain point precisely and is controllable to ship.

Out of scope
The MVP focuses on the pre-purchase comparison decision, deliberately excluding expense tracking, overspend alerts, and in-trip spending — to avoid scope creep and ship on time.

SolutionSolution & key features

  • AI travel-data search engine — integrates the Google Gemini API with Search grounding, streaming back attractions / tickets / passes / transit options to keep data fresh.
  • Visual itinerary planner — a drag-and-drop two-column UI (resource library on the left, multi-day itinerary on the right), with pass-coverage hints, multi-city tabs, and per-day transit settings.
  • Fully automatic comparison engine — on any itinerary change, the backend recomputes every purchase combination against a "buy-everything-separately" baseline (800ms debounce to avoid overload).
  • AI smart summary — turns the structured comparison into a natural-language "conclusion + reasons + differences", flags the best-value option, and shows terms & last-updated reminders.
  • URL as single source of truth (SSOT) — core state (location / days / items) lives in the URL, so itineraries are shareable, bookmarkable, and restored on return.
  • Internationalisation — Traditional Chinese / English / Japanese, with AI output matching the interface language.

User JourneyUser journey

I broke the end-to-end path from "landing to purchase decision" into 8 stages, so the UI, front-end state, API calls, and AI summary all stay aligned to the core flow.

A

Landing

Grasp the value proposition; enter a city at a prominent search entry.

B

Search

AI grounding returns a structured resource list with source count & timestamp.

C

Select

Tap or drag items into the itinerary with instant visual feedback.

D

Plan

Drag to reorder across days; set per-day transit counts.

E

Auto-compare

Any change recomputes (800ms debounce), outputting a structured comparison.

F

Summarize

Stream a conclusion + reasons; flag the best-value option.

G

Buy

Check terms / validity, then jump to the purchase link.

H

Share

URL is the state — shareable, bookmarkable, editable on return.

ArchitectureSystem architecture

The front-end and AI engine are deeply integrated; comparison runs through a rule engine that outputs structured results, which the AI then turns into readable advice — a progressive experience where the table arrives first and the summary follows.

Success MetricsSuccess metrics

  • Task completion rate — share of users who build an itinerary with ≥3 attractions and view the comparison.
  • AI-value adoption — share who open the "best-value" detail, or satisfaction with the AI summary.
  • Data accuracy — user-reported price/pass errors below 0.5%.
  • Engagement — drag interactions per session and "add a day" frequency.
